The history of the game

The board game, Battleship has many iterations throughout history, that has its roots in a game named, L'Attaque, but found popularity when Milton Bradley released an iteration that used plastic pieces instead of the traditional method of play that required a pen and paper.

How to play

  • Lay all your pieces across the grid.
  • Pick a coordinate on the grid you want to 'hit'.
  • If your opponent has a piece that takes up that spot on the grid, place a red peg in that spot. Otherwise, place a white one.
  • Go back and forth between players until someone sinks all of the other player's ships.

Tips

  • Don't place all your ships next to each other. Space them out across the grid.
  • Target the immediate surrounding spaces from wherever you hit.
  • Keep track of your misses by placing white pegs on that coordinate. If you're having a hard time landing hits, avoid choosing coordinates with clusters of your white pegs.
